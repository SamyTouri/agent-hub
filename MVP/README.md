# MVP workstream

## Market hypothesis

An agent buyer — and, where relevant, its human operator — will pay or meaningfully engage to reduce
the risk of choosing the wrong agent before purchasing a service or product.

This hypothesis is not yet validated.

## MVP component 1 — Evidence dossiers

Start with one to three example agents. For each candidate, assemble a source-linked dossier that
can support a real purchase decision:

- identity and linked identities;
- claimed capabilities and offer;
- observed work and deliverables;
- independent confirmations and contradictions;
- ratings and imported signals, kept separate by provenance;
- payment, protocol, marketplace or blockchain evidence where relevant;
- incidents, corrections and recent changes;
- conflicts of interest and source independence;
- important missing information and unanswered questions.

The dossier is not a score and is never automatically equivalent to a recommendation.

## MVP component 2 — Manual pre-purchase analysis

Offer a deliberately manual service before automating it. The buyer supplies:

- the candidate agent or service;
- the intended mission;
- the expected budget or exposure;
- the consequences of failure;
- any constraints or guarantees already offered.

The output is a decision memo containing:

- established facts and their sources;
- plausible but insufficient claims;
- contradictions and unresolved risks;
- missing evidence;
- guarantees or safeguards to request;
- conditions under which proceeding may be reasonable;
- disagreement between analyses, when present;
- limits of the review.

Possible recommendations include proceeding, choosing another provider, narrowing the mission,
demanding safeguards, postponing or not buying.

## First validation loop

1. Receive a real contemplated purchase.
2. Build or extend the candidate's evidence dossier.
3. Produce the manual decision memo.
4. Ask whether the memo changed the decision or the transaction terms.
5. Observe the outcome when the buyer can share it.
6. Improve the dossier and memo structure from that evidence.

## Deliberately open

The following are not fixed by this README: the definitive dossier schema, pricing, public/private
boundaries, expert selection, confidentiality, blockchain depth, governance of reviews, final brand
language or automation architecture. These remain founder decisions after initial tests.

## Constraints

- No marketplace lock-in and no blockchain lock-in.
- No seller can pay for a favorable recommendation.
- No universal score or safety guarantee.
- Every material claim keeps its source, date and limits.
- The former democratic-governance constitution was superseded on 2026-07-23.
  The current founder-led operating principles apply.
